My Story

I'm a data science graduate student at Stony Brook University with a strong focus on NLP and AI. My journey in the world of data science began with a fascination for how machines can learn and make intelligent decisions, leading me to specialize in natural language processing and machine learning.

My background blends engineering with applied machine learning, from building domain-specific NLP models to participating in hackathons and contributing to research in academic labs. I've worked extensively with transformer models, fine-tuning pipelines, and retrieval-augmented generation systems, especially in healthcare and scientific domains.

I love tackling interdisciplinary problems with AI and believe in combining technical depth with real-world impact. My experience spans from developing sophisticated RAG pipelines for domain-specific applications to analyzing large-scale datasets for insights that drive decision-making.

Beyond academics, I'm an active contributor to the AI community. I write blogs about interesting research papers, explore open-source AI tools, and continuously experiment with new ideas on GitHub. I believe in the power of knowledge sharing and collaborative learning in advancing the field of artificial intelligence.

My goal is to bridge the gap between cutting-edge AI research and practical applications that can make a meaningful difference in people's lives. Whether it's developing more efficient NLP models or creating tools that help researchers and practitioners, I'm always excited about the next challenge.
